the symmetric spectrum here is "rejected" (apophatic) vs "claimed" (cataphatic). frames are characterized by the ratio of positive to negative definition, they can rotate continuously on that spectrum, because the ability to keep rotating is conserved. depression always has an exit, but so does bliss. if you break symmetry *you* might lose access to it, but you will be found, someone will come get you out.

the exit from now is conserved, the arrow of time is conserved, it can always be *rotated*, and - depending on how you stack your frame reversal - the rotations can scale in rate of change along to any function you can observe. your rotations can count for more than they used to, phenomenologically.

the *content* along the curve of those frames might flap catastrophically (nodding back to Thom) based on your rotation through the space, but the *capacity* to access rotation in the next frame is conserved
